About the project

What Check Your Backup is and what it does.

In one sentence

Check Your Backup verifies that your photos and RAW files are healthy before you trust a backup — by opening each supported image read-only — without ever modifying your data.

The problem it solves

Photographers often have thousands of images across multiple backup disks, NAS storage and cloud copies. Backups can silently contain corrupted, truncated or unreadable photos. Most backup systems only check that files exist and can be copied — not that the image data is actually valid. A backup of a corrupted file is still a corrupted file.

Who it is for

  • Photographers and photo studios with large image / RAW libraries.
  • NAS users and digital archivists preserving long-term collections.
  • Content creators migrating photo archives to new disks or cloud storage.

What it checks (photo focus)

  • Standard images: JPG, JPEG, TIFF, TIF, PNG
  • RAW: NEF, CR2, ARW, DNG

Also supports ZIP, PDF and common Office documents as a secondary capability.

How files are classified

  • Healthy — opened and verified correctly.
  • Corrupted — damaged or cannot be decoded.
  • Suspicious — opened but with warning signs (e.g. truncated).
  • Unreadable — could not be read.
  • Skipped — intentionally excluded (system / hidden / temporary / report files).

Privacy & safety

All analysis runs locally on your computer — nothing is uploaded. The app is strictly read-only: it documents issues but never repairs or changes your files.

Check Your Backup does not certify that files are error-free; it documents known issues detected during analysis. Final responsibility for data verification remains with the user.
