About the project

What Check Your Backup is and what it does.

In one sentence

Check Your Backup verifies that your files are healthy before you trust a backup — by opening each supported file read-only — without ever modifying your data.

The problem it solves

Backups can silently contain corrupted, truncated or missing files. Most backup systems only check that files exist and can be copied — not that their contents are actually valid. You often find out too late. Check Your Backup opens and validates files so you catch problems up front.

Who it is for

  • Photographers and creatives with large image / RAW libraries.
  • Anyone migrating data to a new disk, NAS or cloud.
  • People who keep long-term archives and want proof their backups are intact.

What it checks

  • Images: JPG, TIFF, PNG
  • RAW: CR2, NEF, ARW, DNG
  • Archives: ZIP (also analyzes images inside the archive)
  • Documents: PDF, DOCX, XLSX, PPTX

How files are classified

  • Healthy — opened and verified correctly.
  • Corrupted — damaged or cannot be decoded.
  • Suspicious — opened but with warning signs (e.g. truncated).
  • Unreadable — could not be read.
  • Skipped — intentionally excluded (system / hidden / temporary / report files).

Privacy & safety

All analysis runs locally on your computer — nothing is uploaded. The app is strictly read-only: it documents issues but never repairs or changes your files.

Check Your Backup does not certify that files are error-free; it documents known issues detected during analysis. Final responsibility for data verification remains with the user.
